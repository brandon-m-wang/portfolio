k-NN Parkinson's Classifier

This is a simple lazy-learning k-NN classifier built from training data provided by a 2018 comparative analysis of speech processing algorithms led by various researchers in Istanbul. Historically, around 90% of diagnosed PD patients had demonstrated some type of vocal disorder in earlier stages of the disease's progression. The study the classifier is centered around leverages signal processing algorithms taken from simple speech recordings to provide clinically feasible information. Patients are asked to sustain vowel phonations, while signal processing algorithms map out hyper-specific attributes. Of them are Time Frequency Features (TFF), Mel Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs), Wavelet Transform based Features, Vocal Fold Features and TWQT. What this classifier seeks to achieve is a means of accurately predicting whether or not someone has Parkinson's Disease from a simple voice recording (provided all the data is extractable) in the early stages. Since there is no cure or treatment for PD, the best treatment is ultimately early diagnosis. Currently ~85% accuracy using a holdout validation scheme. Data taken from UCI's Machine Learning Repository, along with various departments from Bahcesehir University, Yildiz Technical University, and Istanbul University.

Safeguard Seattle

Protecting marginalized communities from hate crimes in Seattle through an interactive heat map. Safeguard Seattle is a tool meant to help marginalized communities avoid being victims of potential hate crimes. SGS uses up to date police reports to create an interactive heat map that tracks the location and frequency of hate crimes across Seattle. Users are able to filter incidents of hate crime by various identity categories. Depending on the user's chosen race/ethnicity, gender, sexuality, and religion, the heat map redistributes itself to display the frequency and location of incidents by the user's chosen criteria.

Grassroots for Housing

Combatting racial and economic housing segregation in Austin with activism and awareness. The primary reason that allows for regressive policies and discriminatory housing infrastructures to remain in place is the simple lack of awareness and subsequent willingness to take action. The only way to fix this issue of segregational zoning is to pressure policy-makers to vote for change beginning at the local (or grassroots) level. The platform is aimed at not only helping the economically challenged and displaced to find their next month-to-month living space, but also educating the general population about the geopolitical history and issues that have resulted from redlining and subsequent zoning policies and to provide avenues for change.

Postmint

Postmint allows users to log in with Instagram accounts, and then they can upload various product images to the site and select how often they want to post. Then, a machine learning API was used to associate the images to product names. After that, trend information for all of the products were scraped, and normalized the data and choose the product with the highest z-score for that day compared to the set of trend data over the last 5 years. When a post is supposed to be posted, the product that has the highest relative interest for the day is found, and then a comment and hashtags to post are generated automatically, and posted to the user.

Educe

COVID-19 has pushed education online for communities globally, which has had many negative impacts on students. The most drastic of which is that students without access to their own devices can't attend school with their peers. In addition to this, there is a big problem currently with recycling old computers. This project allows for students in need of certain tech for their distance learning to register for an account and make posts requesting old devices. The active posts are then shown to people looking at the site. If somebody is browsing the platform and has an old device that is being requested, they can contact the student in need via email to set up shipping logistics.

Wildfire Watch

A mobile application that utilizes NASA satellite heatmap imagery to map out a self-updating wildfire distribution and an algorithm that determines the size and threat levels of certain fires with regards to relative user location. WW allows users to view where wildfires were in the past 24 hours. When a user is in a dangerous vicinity to a wildfire, the app notifies the user to evacuate. If the app determines that the user is trapped in a fire, it will immediately send messages to first responders about the user's current location.

Mindfull

A wireframe that proposes the integration of smartphone accelerometer technology and FDA-approved neurological assessment batteries to create a means of achieving an early diagnosis of Parkinson's Disease in at-risk individuals through tremor detection and cognitive health monitoring. Mindfull seeks to provide those at risk of Parkinson's with an outlet to engage themselves with while simultaneously tracking a "utility index" which is derived from several assessments made on the user as they interact with the application. Whether it's cognitively stimulating puzzles or tremor assessments based off of smartphone accelerometers, Mindfull seeks to aid in the early diagnosis of Parkinson's disease, and ultimately improve health outcomes.
